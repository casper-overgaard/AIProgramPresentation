01 · Context

Why now.

AI is already changing two things at the same time — what we build and how we work. If we don’t get ahead of it, three things are likely to happen.

01

Local & out of sync

Workflow optimisation happens locally and out of sync across teams.

02

Experiences drift

AI experiences drift apart across products and touchpoints — quality and context handling stop matching.

03

Others set the shape

Adjacent functions end up defining the future way of working for us.

04

Surfaces multiply

More AI-enabled surfaces, interfaces and interaction points are coming. Without shared patterns, they drift early and become harder to reconnect later.

Bottom line · we need a practical model that helps us align, build capability, and learn through real work.

Bootcamp · in depth

Six weeks, real cases, manager-ratified time.

Selection The Compact Six weeks Tier placement Signals

Selection

Manager-nominated, paired with a 100-word self-statement from the nominee. Both required.

  • No nomination without a statement — no statement without a nomination.
  • Manager commits to protecting 4–6 hrs/week in writing before the participant is confirmed.
  • Filters out the conscripted; creates a small investment moment.
  • Cohorts sized at 10–12 — enough for cross-spoke ties, small enough for debriefs to work.
  • Two cohorts run in sequence — Cohort 1 first, Cohort 2 follows; lessons sharpen the second pass.

The Compact

A one-pager signed by participant, manager, and facilitator before Week 0. Six commitments:

  • Time. 4–6 hrs/week for six active weeks. Manager-protected.
  • Deliverables. Six weekly submissions. Missing two in a row triggers a 1:1 and a continue-or-step-back conversation.
  • Real work. The applied artefact is a real piece of the participant’s product team work, not a sandbox exercise. The product team sees it.
  • Confidentiality. What’s said in debriefs stays in debriefs. Deliverables shared with manager and facilitator only.
  • Placement. Tier placement is developmental, not a performance review input.
  • Dropout. One free step-back to Cohort 2 if life intervenes. A second step-back ends eligibility this cycle.

The six weeks

Each active week follows the same shape — plenary, applied exercise in own work context, small-group debrief, deliverable. Anchor reading comes in mid-week and is kept short.

Week 0
Kickoff (90 min, live)
Focus
Cohort meets. Tone is set. The Compact is signed in the room.
Format
Six 15-min blocks — framing, cohort intros, TA intros, walkthrough, buddy pairing, Compact signing.
Deliverable
Signed Compact and a confirmed buddy pair across spokes.
Week 1
Land the tools
Focus
Hands-on texture with Claude, Cursor, Figma Make. No theory.
Plenary
One real task end-to-end across the three tools, narrating prompting decisions and leaving the wrong turns visible.
Hands-on
Pick two of three — an audience-shifting summary in Claude; a brief-to-screen exercise in Figma Make; a component explanation and improvement suggestion in Cursor.
Deliverable
Two-paragraph reflection plus one chosen output you would defend.
Week 2
Production is no longer the bottleneck
Focus
Reframe what your job is when production is cheap.
Plenary
Discussion-led. Anchored in Sziebert (Kaizen of Context) and Chaturvedi (New Design Leverage). Active question — what’s the bottleneck in your sprint that AI doesn’t touch?
Hands-on
Identify a specific bottleneck in your current sprint. Redo one weekly recurring task with AI in the loop. Measure time and quality before vs. after.
Deliverable
Bottleneck statement plus a before/after note with rough time data.
Week 3
Where design happens now
Focus
The “design process is dead” debate, and what it means for your week.
Plenary
Three-way debate — Wen’s “process is dead”, Bjørnhaug’s “process relocates”, NN/G’s “compressed, not dead”. Participants take a side.
Hands-on
Map your team’s real design process — not the official Confluence diagram, the actual flow. Mark where AI could relocate a decision, and where it could make the decision worse.
Deliverable
Real-process map plus one redone artefact using the “decision relocated” frame.
Week 4
Teams, roles, and the new shapes
Focus
What “designer” means in 18 months. Aptitudes, not titles.
Plenary
Cross-cutting view from Darmour, Reyes, Cyr, and the industry reports. Aptitudes 2×2 — judgment depth × build leverage. Contained counter-narrative debate.
Hands-on
Write 250 words on your 18-month role. Then have one conversation with your manager about it. The most uncomfortable homework, and the highest-leverage.
Deliverable
250-word role-future note plus a one-paragraph manager-conversation reflection.
Week 5
Ship something real
Focus
Apply everything in your own product team. The proof.
Plenary
Replaced with a 90-min mid-week working session. Participants bring their case; the cohort critiques in flight.
Hands-on (4–6 hrs)
Pick one task from your current sprint. Do it with AI in the loop, end-to-end. The bar — “I could put this in front of my PM and EM and defend it.”
Deliverable
Applied artefact plus one piece of real feedback from inside the product team plus a 100-word reflection.
Week 6
Showcase and placement
Focus
Land the readiness conversation. Earn the placement.
Showcase
90 min. Each participant gets 5 min to show their applied artefact plus 1 min on “what changed in how I work”. Cross-cohort attendance encouraged.
Placement
30-min 1:1 with manager and facilitator. Tier placement on the readiness scorecard.
Follow-up
Same self-assessment scored again at Day 30. Did the gains stick? Captured as one data point per participant.

Tier placement

Placement is decided in a 30-min 1:1 with the participant, their manager, and the facilitator. The conversation is developmental — here’s where you are, here’s what would move you up.

  • Explorer. Has the floor. Can use the tools. Hasn’t yet shifted day-to-day workflow.
  • Emerging. Has integrated AI into one or two recurring workflows.
  • Accelerator-eligible. Could credibly serve as a champion in a future cohort.
  • Advanced. Already operates at the highest tier. Rare for Bootcamp graduates, but possible.

What disqualifies a tier-up:

  • Two missed deliverables without a stepped-back conversation.
  • Applied artefact was a sandbox or wasn’t used.
  • No manager-conversation reflection from Week 4 — the structural shift didn’t happen.

Signals the cohort worked

Measured at Week 6 and again at Day 30.

  • ≥ 80% submit all six deliverables.
  • ≥ 70% of applied artefacts referenced or used by the receiving product team within 30 days.
  • ≥ 60% move up one tier on the readiness picture.
  • ≥ 70% report one or more recurring workflow now AI-integrated.
  • ≥ 4 graduates volunteer to TA the next cohort.
  • Manager “would-nominate-again” rate above 70%.
