01 · Context

Why now.

AI is already changing two things at the same time — what we build and how we work. If we don’t get ahead of it, three things are likely to happen.

01

Local & out of sync

Workflow optimisation happens locally and out of sync across teams.

02

Experiences drift

AI experiences drift apart across products and touchpoints — quality and context handling stop matching.

03

Others set the shape

Adjacent functions end up defining the future way of working for us.

04

Surfaces multiply

More AI-enabled surfaces, interfaces and interaction points are coming. Without shared patterns, they drift early and become harder to reconnect later.

Bottom line · we need a practical model that helps us align, build capability, and learn through real work.
01 · Context

The problem is two shifts at once.

One reshapes what we build. The other reshapes how we work. The programme has to address both.

Shift 01

AI-enabled experiences

What we build is changing — interfaces that are context-aware, with AI running inside them.

  • Automation
  • Orchestration
  • Human oversight
  • Context-aware interfaces

Shift 02

AI-assisted ways of working

How we work is changing — new handoffs, faster cycles, and the shape of roles compressing and shifting.

  • New handoffs
  • Faster cycles
  • Role compression
  • Role change
This needs to address both customer-facing AI quality and internal AI-enabled practice.

02 · Programme · Leg 01

AI Experience & Enablement Group

A small working group to compare what is already happening, clarify what good looks like, and reduce drift early.

Purpose

Compare what’s already happening, clarify what good looks like, and reduce drift early.

Origin CEED-initiated
Shape Cross-functional
Size Small & selected
  • —AI-enabled ways of working.
  • —Customer-facing AI patterns and quality.
  • —Response quality and contextual coherence across touchpoints.
  • —Where agentic thinking makes sense — and where it does not.
  • —Shared principles, examples, and likely pilot areas.
  • —CEED-initiated — core: Design, Product, Engineering / Architecture.
  • —Selected adjacent: likely Ocean and L&S representatives where workflow, handoff, or customer surface area overlaps.
  • —Small and curated — not a broad community at the start.
  • —Leaders will ask how the Enablement Group relates to a potential AI UX Chapter. The short answer: they are different things and not alternatives to each other.
  • —An AI UX Chapter is a design-led community that shapes UX vision, craft standards and maturity — it operates within the design function.
  • —The Group is the cross-functional practical layer where design, product and engineering align on what good looks like in shipped work — it cannot live inside one function.
  • —Both can exist. Worth agreeing how they sit next to each other before the Group runs its first session.
  • —A small, trusted group.
  • —The place where AI practice gets compared and sharpened.
  • —Discussion turned into action.
02 · Programme · Leg 02

Bootcamp / Readiness Track

Bring CEED UX colleagues with little AI exposure to a credible working baseline — through real Maersk cases, not exercises.

Purpose

Lift the floor across CEED UX in a practical way — through real work, not just talk about the future.

Audience CEED UX, Explorer tier
Length 6 weeks + kickoff
Cohorts 2 × 10–12
Time 4–6 hrs / week
  • —Lift the Explorer floor — bring colleagues with little exposure to a credible working baseline.
  • —Counter the equity risk — nobody left behind once the Group and Pods start moving.
  • —Make readiness visible — graduates land on a four-tier picture (Explorer / Emerging / Accelerator / Advanced).
  • —Real work, not training exercises — every week ends with something usable in the participant’s product team.
  • —Six active weeks, plus an async pre-week and a live kickoff.
  • —Each week — plenary (75 min) + applied exercise (2–3 hrs on own work) + small-group debrief (45 min).
  • —Cross-spoke buddy pairings, a mid-week pulse reading, drop-in office hours.
  • —4–6 hrs per participant per week — manager-ratified upfront via a signed Compact.
  • —CEED UX designers with little to no working AI exposure.
  • —Manager-nominated, paired with a 100-word self-statement from the nominee.
  • —Two parallel cohorts of 10–12. Cohort 1 first, Cohort 2 follows.
  • —Not for those already shipping AI-assisted work, and not for cross-functional roles.
  • —Week 0 · Kickoff — the cohort meets, the Compact is signed.
  • —Week 1 · Tools — Claude, Cursor, Figma Make on real tasks.
  • —Week 2 · Bottleneck — production isn’t the constraint anymore; what is?
  • —Week 3 · Process — where design decisions actually get made now.
  • —Week 4 · Roles — aptitudes, and a hard conversation with your manager.
  • —Week 5 · Ship — one real artefact, defended inside the product team.
  • —Week 6 · Showcase — tier placement and a 30-day follow-up.
  • —Most participants move up one tier on the readiness picture.
  • —Most applied artefacts are picked up by the receiving product team.
  • —A pipeline forms — graduates ready to TA the next cohort.
  • —Movement is visible across spokes, not concentrated in one.

03 · Execution

What the first phase could look like.

Sequenced to shape the model with real work, not in the abstract.

Start Month 1 Month 2 Month 3+
Month 01 Shape

Form & frame

Set the working shape before running anything.

  • Form the AI Experience & Enablement Group
  • Run a short signal-mapping survey before the first Group session — what work is in motion, where blockers keep showing up, where alignment feels most needed
  • Identify what good looks like at a very early level
  • Shape the first version of the Bootcamp
  • Identify pilot candidates
  • Name the first practical outputs: 3–5 principles, 1 Bootcamp outline, 1 readiness lens, 1–2 pod pilots
Month 02 Run

Stand up & test

Put weight on the model in practice.

  • Run the first Group sessions
  • Lock the first Bootcamp shape
  • Identify first participants and readiness signals
  • Stand up the first pod / pilot where timing allows
Month 03+ Refine

Learn & refine

Let real evidence reshape the model.

  • Continue the Group as practical alignment layer
  • Run or test the first Bootcamp version
  • Capture lessons from pods
  • Refine what good looks like based on actual evidence
  • Keep guardrails active — Group stays small, one pilot at a time, loops stay short, relevance over representation
